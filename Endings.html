Ending #1 (WORST)

This ending is achieved by not killing Caracass and not giving the gun to Purnell. Due to his untrust toward the Doctor, his therapy fails and he concludes that life is meaningless and grows resentful toward the world around him. In a blind and selfish rage, he attempts to murder everyone in sight; including Sophie and Doctor Purnell. Before committing suicide, he leaves a sour note describing his hatred for everything around him, and that if it weren't for his disability he'd kill more people.

Ending #2 (BAD)

Achieved by not killing Caracass and giving the gun to Purnell, Simon gives in to Purnell's support but deems it futile, and that life is pointless. Unable to move past the rejection he endured, he murders his love-interest Sophie in spite of her actions. In his suicide note, he thanks Purnell for attempting to help him.

Ending #3 (SAD)

Achieved by killing Caracass and not giving the gun to Purnell, it is Ending #2 reversed. He forgives Sophie for rejecting him and moves past the situation. He regards Sophie as being the only person to ever truly care about him. During Simon's treatment, his attemp to cope via writing in his book worsens his mental state by "making him realize how broken he was" and murders Purnell for revenge. He leaves a suicide note in a sorrowful tone as to not let anyone show Sophie what he did.

Ending #4 (BEST)

Achieved by killing Caracass and giving the gun to Purnell, real Simon works against his counterpart in a gunfight before shooting book Simon during the showdown, encapsulating his departure from his visage, and letting go from the stress and trauma. However, during this hallucinatory showdown he shoots and kills two police officers in a PTSD-induced episode. He gets the necessary mental help from Purnell and spends the rest of his life in a mental hospital surrounded by people he loves, with Sophie frequently visiting him.
